What is Blue, Green, and Grey Water?

  • Blue Water: Freshwater sourced from rivers, lakes, and aquifers used for irrigation, industrial, and domestic purposes. Overuse can cause water scarcity and ecosystem damage.
  • Green Water: Rainwater stored in soil and used directly by plants. Vital for rainfed agriculture, it is the natural soil moisture supporting crops.
  • Grey Water: The amount of freshwater required to dilute pollutants from agricultural activities to meet water quality standards, reflecting water pollution impact.
